Safety & Reporting — Child Safety (CSAE)

Public page describing policies, reporting flow and technical measures to prevent child sexual abuse and exploitation.

Policy Report Controls

Public Policy Summary

We prohibit any content that sexualizes minors or facilitates sexual exploitation. This includes CSAM, grooming, and instructions for illegal sexual activity involving minors.

Prohibited Content

  • Images or videos of sexual acts involving minors (CSAM).
  • Sexual descriptions, encouragement, or instructions involving minors.
  • Grooming, solicitation, or facilitation of abuse.

Allowed Content (Examples)

  • Non‑sexual references to minors (e.g., education or parenting).
  • Educational material about child protection and safety.
  • Legal reporting and victim support content.

Note: This page is a public policy summary — legal and internal processes appear further below.

Report an Incident

If you encounter suspected CSAE content, please report it. Do not upload illegal imagery or video through this public form — contact a secure intake channel or law enforcement instead.

Security Contact (Email)

Do not upload illicit files to this form. If you possess evidentiary files, contact law enforcement or a secure intake channel.

Technical Controls & Processes

  1. Automated Detection: perceptual hashing (PDQ/PhotoDNA) and ML classifiers. Low‑confidence matches are escalated to manual review.
  2. Manual Review & Escalation: trained reviewers redact sensitive data and escalate confirmed CSAM to law enforcement.
  3. Minimized Storage: store metadata and hashes when possible; sensitive files stay in restricted, audited environments.
  4. Reporting & Cooperation: workflows to hotlines (NCMEC, national hotlines) and law enforcement based on jurisdiction.

Implementation Guidance for Engineers

  • Never store CSAM in public buckets; use secure intake channels with chain‑of‑custody logging.
  • Compute hashes safely and compare against known‑bad databases.
  • Restrict access and maintain immutable audit logs.
  • Provide reviewer wellbeing support and safe tooling.
